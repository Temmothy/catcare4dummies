Q: At what age can I adopt a kitten?
A: Ideally, kittens should be at least 8 weeks old before adoption. This gives them time to be weaned and to learn important behaviors from their mother and siblings.
Q: What’s the first thing I should do when I bring my kitten home?
A: Set up a safe, quiet space with their litter box, food, water, toys, and a comfy bed. Let them explore slowly and get used to the environment at their own pace.
Q: How often should I feed my kitten?
A: Kittens under 6 months should eat 3–4 times a day. Use a high-quality kitten formula with plenty of protein and fat to support growth.
Q: Can I give my kitten regular milk?
A: Nope! Cow’s milk can upset their stomach. Stick with clean water and kitten-specific milk replacer if they’re very young.
Q: When should I take my kitten to the vet?
A: Within the first few days of bringing them home. The vet will check for any health issues, start vaccinations, and plan a wellness schedule.
Q: What vaccinations do kittens need?
A: Core vaccines include FVRCP (feline viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia) and rabies. Your vet will create a schedule based on age and health.
Q: When should I spay or neuter my kitten?
A: Usually between 4 to 6 months, but your vet will give you the best timing based on your kitten’s development.
Q: How do I litter train my kitten?
A: Most kittens naturally take to the litter box. Place them in it after meals or naps, and keep it clean. Use unscented, non-clumping litter at first.
Q: How can I tell if my kitten is healthy?
A: Bright eyes, clean ears, steady weight gain, regular eating and pooping, and playful behavior are good signs. Any changes—like diarrhea or lethargy—should be checked by a vet.
Q: Should I bathe my kitten?
A: Only if necessary. Most kittens clean themselves well. If they get into something messy, use a warm, damp cloth or a gentle kitten shampoo.
Q: How do I introduce my kitten to other pets?
A: Slowly! Start with scent swapping, then visual introductions, and finally supervised interactions. Be patient—it can take days or even weeks.
